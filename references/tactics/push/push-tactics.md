# Push Tactics — Paid Acquisition, Affiliates, Direct Sales

Source: Bronson Taylor, Growth Hacking Playbook Module 3

## Purchase Ads

### Core Economics

- **UAC (User Acquisition Cost)** = Total Ad Spend / Number of Users Acquired
- UAC must be lower than LTV or the channel bleeds cash
- Optimize for 10x improvements (new channels, new angles, new audiences) over 1% incremental tweaks — most companies waste time split-testing button colors when the entire channel is wrong

### 7 Psychological Triggers for Ads

Apply to every ad creative and landing page:

1. **Reciprocity** — Give something first (free guide, tool, sample) so the prospect feels obligated to engage
2. **Commitment/Consistency** — Get a small "yes" first (quiz, micro-signup) then escalate the ask
3. **Social Proof** — Show numbers, testimonials, logos, user counts — people follow crowds
4. **Authority** — Display credentials, press logos, expert endorsements
5. **Liking** — Use relatable faces, stories, language that mirrors the audience
6. **Scarcity** — Limited spots, countdown timers, "only X left" — urgency drives action
7. **Unity** — Shared identity ("fellow founders," "as a parent") creates tribal belonging

### Case Studies

- **Gym Shark** — $128M built primarily on influencer-driven paid ads. Found micro-influencers in fitness, gave free product, paid for sponsored posts. Scaled spend proportional to influencer performance.
- **Gravity Blanket** — $35M via Kickstarter campaign amplified by targeted Facebook ads. Used the campaign itself as social proof (funded amount displayed in ad copy).
- **PayPal** — $10 signup bonus + $10 referral bonus. Cost per acquisition seemed high but drove 10% daily user growth. The paid referral mechanism was the growth engine — they spent money to make money and accepted short-term losses for network effect dominance.

## Affiliates

### Why Affiliates Work

- Affiliate-referred leads convert **4.4x better** than organic leads (they arrive pre-sold by a trusted source)
- **92% of consumers trust recommendations from friends** over any other form of advertising (Nielsen)
- Zero upfront cost — you pay on performance only (CPA, rev share, or hybrid)

### Shopify Case Study

- Shopify grew to **$17B** largely through its affiliate program
- Affiliates (bloggers, course creators, YouTubers) became the de facto sales force
- Commission structure was generous enough to incentivize real effort: recurring revenue share, not one-time payouts

### Commission Structuring

- Pay enough that affiliates prioritize you over competitors
- Recurring commissions (% of subscription) create long-term alignment
- Tiered structures reward top performers and create aspiration
- Provide affiliates with assets: landing pages, email swipes, banner ads, tracking links

### Recruitment Strategy

1. **Recruit customers first** — they already believe in the product, their endorsement is authentic
2. **Expand to niche influencers** — micro-influencers with engaged audiences in your vertical
3. **Then scale to professional affiliates** — affiliate marketers, comparison sites, review blogs
4. Never start with professional affiliates — they promote anything and add no trust signal

## Direct Sales

### Bronson's Origin Story

- Started by physically visiting local businesses, pitching growth services door-to-door
- No product-market fit signal is stronger than someone paying you face-to-face
- Cold outreach forces you to articulate value in one sentence — if you can't, the product isn't ready

### Uber's City Launch Protocol

From Ryan Graves interview — operational playbook for each new city:

1. Hire a local city launcher (GM)
2. Recruit initial supply (drivers) through direct outreach — Craigslist, limo companies, town car services
3. Seed demand through events, partnerships, promo codes
4. Hit critical density (enough drivers that wait times drop below threshold)
5. Let network effects take over — word of mouth accelerates once experience is consistently good

### Seed Growth Concept

- The layer **beneath** Product-Market Fit
- Before you have PMF, you still need users to test with — Seed Growth is the manual, unscalable work to get those first users
- Examples: hand-delivering the product, personal onboarding calls, concierge service
- This is NOT a growth channel — it's a validation mechanism

### Referral Timing

- Request referrals at the **"momentum of yes"** — the moment after a customer says yes, makes a purchase, or expresses satisfaction
- Must happen within **24 hours** of the positive experience
- The longer you wait, the weaker the impulse — enthusiasm decays fast
- Script it: "You seem happy with X — do you know anyone else who could benefit?"

### B2B Marketplace Infiltration

- Go where your target customers already congregate: trade shows, Slack communities, industry forums, LinkedIn groups
- Don't pitch — add value first, then DM individually
- One quality relationship in a niche community can cascade into dozens of introductions
- B2B sales is relationship-compounding: every closed deal should produce 2-3 warm referrals
