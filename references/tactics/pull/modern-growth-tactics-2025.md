**Hat:** WHITE | **Robot:** YES | **Viability:** HIGH

- Automatically route high-intent leads to sales, nurture low-intent leads
- Real-time intent data from tools like Bombora, 6sense, Demandbase
- Tools: 6sense, Clearbit, MadKudu, HubSpot predictive scoring

## Platform-Specific Exploits (2025-2026)

### TikTok Algorithm Tactics
- **Creator Search Insights:** TikTok literally tells you what topics have high search volume but low content supply. Create content filling those gaps.
- **Hook strategy:** 3-5 second hooks determine algorithmic distribution. Full watches boost ranking by 40%.
- **Stitch/Remix trending content:** Algorithm favors content that references popular videos
- **Post at low-competition times** rather than peak hours for better initial distribution

**Hat:** WHITE | **Robot:** PARTIAL | **Viability:** HIGH

### YouTube Shorts Tactics
- **Videos over 40 seconds** get 33% higher engagement than shorter clips
- **5.91% engagement rate** — leads all short-form platforms
- **Average viewer retention:** 73% — algorithm prioritizes Average View Duration over likes/comments
- **Trending audio:** Using popular sounds significantly increases distribution
- Cross-promote Shorts to drive subscribers to long-form content

**Hat:** WHITE | **Robot:** YES | **Viability:** HIGH

### Threads Growth Tactics
- Instagram integration means your IG followers can find you on Threads
- Video posts with text commentary outperform video-only
- The platform rewards authentic, conversational content
- Early mover advantage — Threads is still growing and algorithm is generous
- Cross-post from Instagram for double exposure

**Hat:** WHITE | **Robot:** YES | **Viability:** MEDIUM-HIGH (platform still maturing)

### LinkedIn Algorithm Exploits (2025-2026)
- **Carousel posts** receive disproportionate algorithm favor (highest engagement format)
- **Collaborative articles** boost visibility and authority
- **Document posts** (PDFs) get exceptional distribution
- **Comment strategy:** First comment within 30 min of posting triggers algorithm boost
- **Creator mode** unlocks additional distribution features
- **Newsletter feature** pushes content directly to followers' inboxes

**Hat:** WHITE | **Robot:** YES | **Viability:** HIGH

## Community-Led Growth Strategies

### 1. Build a Community as a Growth Channel
- Communities reduce customer acquisition costs by 32% on average
- Community members convert 22% faster than typical leads
- Community referrals achieve 7.3% conversion rate
- Community-led onboarding reduces churn by 29%
- 73% of users who engage weekly remain active for 12+ months

**Implementation:**
1. Start with a "lighthouse" — small group of ideal users
2. Design contribution paths: lurker > contributor > ambassador
3. Track retention first — advocacy follows
4. Expect 12-18 months for meaningful revenue attribution

**Examples:** Notion Ambassadors, Figma community, HubSpot Academy, Webflow University

**Hat:** WHITE | **Robot:** PARTIAL — Community building requires human investment but grows self-sustaining | **Viability:** HIGH

### 2. Ambassador and Champions Programs
- Identify your most engaged users and formalize their advocacy
- Provide exclusive access, early features, recognition, and sometimes compensation
- Ambassadors create content, answer questions, and evangelize independently
- Notion, Salesforce, and AWS all run successful ambassador programs

**Hat:** WHITE | **Robot:** YES (once established) | **Viability:** HIGH

## Product-Led Growth (PLG) Tactics

### 1. Viral Loops Embedded in Product
Build growth into the product so every user action creates exposure:
- **Collaborative features:** Inviting teammates = growth (Slack, Notion, Figma)
- **Shared outputs:** Product output goes to non-users (Calendly links, Loom videos, Canva designs)
- **Branded free tier:** Free version shows branding to recipients (Mailchimp, Squarespace)
- **Public profiles/pages:** User-created content is indexable (GitHub repos, Notion pages, Substack newsletters)

**Key metrics:** Viral coefficient (K-factor), time to value, product-qualified leads (PQLs)

**Hat:** WHITE | **Robot:** YES — Self-feeding by design | **Viability:** HIGH

### 2. Freemium with Strategic Limits
- Free tier must deliver real value (not just a trial)
- Limits should hit naturally as usage grows (not artificially gated)
- Upgrade triggers should feel like a natural next step, not a wall
- Examples: Slack (message history limit), Notion (guest limits), Figma (project limits)

**Hat:** WHITE | **Robot:** YES | **Viability:** HIGH

### 3. Product-Led Onboarding
- Get user to value moment in under 5 minutes
- Progressive disclosure — don't show everything at once
- Interactive tutorials > documentation
- Personalized onboarding paths based on use case
- Celebrate small wins during onboarding (confetti, congratulations)

**Hat:** WHITE | **Robot:** YES | **Viability:** HIGH

## The 2026 Growth Hacking Thesis

**What has changed:**
1. Privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, FTC 2024 rule) have eliminated many grey/black tactics
2. AI has democratized content creation — volume is no longer a differentiator
3. Platform algorithms are smarter at detecting manipulation
4. Users are more sophisticated and skeptical of growth tricks
5. Community and trust are now competitive moats

**What still works:**
1. Product-led growth with genuine viral mechanics
2. Community-led growth with authentic engagement
3. AI-powered personalization (not AI-powered spam)
4. Programmatic SEO with genuine value-add
5. Developer-first / API-first distribution
6. Strategic cold outreach with real personalization
7. Referral programs with product-native rewards
8. Free tools as lead magnets
9. Content repurposing across platforms (one asset, many formats)
10. Platform-native content that plays to each algorithm's preferences

**The meta-principle:** Growth in 2026 rewards systems over tricks, trust over manipulation, and patience over shortcuts. The companies that win build growth machines — repeatable, self-feeding loops that compound over time.
