## The 7 Psychological Triggers of Growth Hacking

Every growth robot should leverage at least one trigger. The most powerful robots use multiple triggers in sequence.

---

### 1. SELF

People are obsessed with themselves. Tap into this directly.

- Speak to their specific pain points at the top of landing pages
- Personalize messaging ("Your business...", "Your results...")
- Show them data about themselves (reports, scores, assessments)
- Use their name, their industry, their specific situation
- Assessments and quizzes are powerful SELF triggers — people want to learn about themselves

---

### 2. PROGRESS

People are motivated by visible advancement.

- LinkedIn profile completion progress bar (classic example)
- Referral program progress ("3 more friends to unlock premium")
- Achievement milestones, streaks, levels
- Onboarding progress indicators
- Gamification elements that show "how far you've come"
- Visual progress bars, completion percentages, level-up notifications

---

### 3. TIME

Urgency drives action.

- Countdown timers (Rhinolaces.com "countdown sale" dynamically refreshed per holiday)
- Limited-time offers with genuine scarcity
- "Last chance" sequences in email
- Seasonal/event-based urgency
- Deadline-driven pricing (price increases on X date)
- Dynamic urgency that resets per context — not fake timers, but real windows of opportunity

---

### 4. CONTRAST

Relative comparison drives perception.

- $10 vs $30 pricing makes $10 feel like a deal
- Feature comparison tables against competitors
- Popcorn pricing: large only a few cents more than small (anchoring)
- Before/after demonstrations
- "Was $X, now $Y" strike-through pricing
- Three-tier pricing where the middle option is the target (decoy effect)

---

### 5. FEAR

Loss aversion is stronger than gain desire.

- FOMO (fear of missing out)
- Kanye's popup shop: "only here for 2 weeks"
- Fear-based headlines addressing worst-case scenarios
- "What happens if you DON'T act?" framing
- Social proof showing others are already acting (and you're being left behind)
- Limited inventory/availability indicators
- Risk of inaction messaging

---

### 6. SOCIAL PROOF

We follow the herd.

- Testimonials, case studies, reviews
- User counts ("Join 10,000+ customers")
- Client logos, trust badges
- Real-time activity notifications (useproof.com style)
- "Most popular" badges on pricing pages
- Industry-specific proof (same-industry case studies carry more weight)
- Ratings and review aggregates

---

### 7. LOGIC

The rational backup after the emotional decision.

- Feature lists, specifications
- ROI calculators
- Detailed comparison pages (e.g., slack.com/features)
- Data-driven proof points
- Whitepapers, research, third-party validation
- Note: **Logic comes LAST** — people decide emotionally first, then rationalize. Logic is for post-decision justification, not for driving the initial decision.

---

## Application Framework

When implementing triggers:

1. **Pick 1 trigger** you can implement RIGHT NOW
2. **Brainstorm 3-5 ideas** for applying it in the current context
3. **Test the strongest idea** — ship it, don't theorize
4. **Measure impact** — track the specific metric it should move
5. **Add more triggers** to the same flow once the first is validated

Layering triggers is where the real power lives. A landing page using SELF (personalized headline) + SOCIAL PROOF (testimonials) + TIME (limited offer) + LOGIC (ROI calculator) will outperform one using a single trigger.

## The Growth Hacking Funnel

Three stages, each requiring different tactics:

### Stage 1: Get Visitors

Like dating — first impressions. You need to get attention before anything else matters.

**Pull tactics (own audience):**
- Blogging
- Podcasting
- Ebooks / lead magnets
- Social media content
- Webinars

**Pull tactics (leveraged audience):**
- Guest blogging
- Guest podcasting
- Conference speaking
- Guest webinars
- Collaborations

**Push tactics:**
- Paid ads
- Affiliates
- Direct sales / outbound
- Promo swaps

**Product tactics:**
- Network invitations (invite your friends)
- Referral programs
- Social sharing features
- API integrations
- Incentivized sharing

### Stage 2: Activate Members

Like a relationship — building trust. Visitors arrived, now convert them into engaged users.

- Landing page optimization
- Copywriting
- Calls to action
- Onboarding flows
- Gamification
- Pricing strategy
- First-value delivery speed (time to "aha moment")

### Stage 3: Retain Users

Like a marriage — long-term commitment. Activated users must stay and grow in value.

- Ongoing value delivery
- Community building
- Feature development based on usage data
- Re-engagement campaigns
- Loyalty programs
- Customer success touchpoints
- Expansion revenue (upsell, cross-sell)

---

## The 80/20 Rule Applied to the Funnel

Use the Pareto Principle to prioritize:

- **Which funnel stage is the biggest bottleneck?** Focus there.
- Spend 80% of effort on the constrained stage
- Don't optimize activation if you have no traffic
- Don't chase traffic if your activation is broken
- Don't invest in retention if you can't activate

**Diagnose first, then prescribe.** The funnel stages are sequential — a broken earlier stage makes later-stage optimization meaningless.

## T-Shaped Marketer Model

Three layers of skill depth for growth practitioners:

### Layer 1: Base Knowledge
- Design
- Branding
- Analytics
- Coding
- Sales

### Layer 2: Marketing Foundation
- CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization)
- Copywriting
- Funnel Marketing
- Automation

### Layer 3: Channel Expertise
- Facebook/Meta Ads
- Email Marketing
- PR
- Social Media
- Content Marketing
- Partnerships
- Virality Engineering
- Retention Systems

**Growth path:** Go deep (T-shaped) in 2 skills first. Then broaden to V-shaped (3-4 deep skills). Then U-shaped (broad competence with multiple deep areas) over time.

## Growth Hacking Definition

> "Growth hacking is when you combine marketing, psychology and technology to achieve business growth."
> — Bronson Taylor

**Formula:** traditional marketing + psychology × technology

The multiplication by technology is what separates growth hacking from traditional marketing. Technology doesn't just add — it **multiplies** the effect. A single psychological trigger applied manually has linear impact. The same trigger deployed through automated technology has exponential reach.

## CGA Application Notes

When using these frameworks in growth advisory:

- **Always diagnose the funnel bottleneck first** before recommending tactics
- **Layer multiple triggers** in every growth robot — single-trigger robots underperform
- **SELF and SOCIAL PROOF** are the two most universally applicable triggers
- **LOGIC is always last** — never lead with features and specs
- **The 7 triggers map directly to robot design** — each step in a robot should leverage at least one trigger
- **Modern tools make trigger implementation faster** — personalization engines, social proof widgets, countdown tools, and AI-powered copy all accelerate deployment
