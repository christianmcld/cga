## The 4 Ingredients of Virality

Every viral campaign must satisfy ALL FOUR:

1. **Emotion** — If the content doesn't make you FEEL something (anger, surprise, laughter, disgust, awe), nobody shares it. Indifference = death.

2. **Originality** — Must be innovative or surprising. Something that hasn't been done before, or a new twist on something familiar.

3. **On Trend** — It's always easier to continue a conversation than to start one. Find what's already being discussed in media and creatively attach your campaign to it.

4. **Click Potential** — THIS IS THE REAL METRIC. Content is NOT king — click potential reigns. Journalists only write about things that will get clicks. Their KPI is traffic. Their business model is ads. They need clicks. Give them a reason to believe your story will get clicks.

### Why Click Potential Matters Most
Blogs and publishers make money from ads. Journalists are judged on views and shares, not writing quality. This means:
- They'll only write about you if they believe people will click
- Trending topics are "safe" — editors know they'll get clicks
- This is why the Kardashians are always in the news — guaranteed clicks
- Your job: convince editors that YOUR story will get THEIR readers to click

## The Angle Machine

The same campaign can be pitched to different outlets with completely different emotional angles:

| Outlet Type | Angle | Example |
|-------------|-------|---------|
| Tech/innovation blogs | "This is genius" | "Check out this revolutionary approach to X" |
| Entertainment/gossip | "This is hilarious/outrageous" | "You won't believe what this person did" |
| Advocacy/cause blogs | "This is disgusting/wrong" | "This person is exploiting X" |
| Career/business blogs | "This is smart business" | "This entrepreneur found a new way to..." |
| General interest | "This is surprising" | "Did you hear about..." |

**Key principle:** You're not lying about the content. You're framing the SAME content to evoke different emotions for different audiences. Every product/campaign has multiple emotional angles — the growth hacker's job is to identify and weaponize each one.

**Use alias accounts to pitch different angles.** You can't be the "genius inventor" and the "outraged citizen" simultaneously from the same email.

## The Alias Email System

**Core rule: Never email journalists as yourself asking for coverage.**

### Why Aliases Work
1. **Journalists hate pitch emails** — they get hundreds daily. Another "write about my amazing product" email gets deleted.
2. **Self-promotion kills credibility** — if YOU say your product is amazing, nobody cares. If a "random person" tips them off, it feels like a discovery.
3. **Three follow-ups instead of two** — alias intro → your follow-up → alias follow-up. Triple the touchpoints without looking desperate.
4. **Social guilt** — when a third party is CC'd, ignoring the email means ignoring TWO people. The social pressure to respond increases significantly.

### The Alias Email Template

**From:** Fake alias (e.g., "Jane Morton")
**To:** journalist@publication.com
**CC:** yourealemail@gmail.com
**Subject:** Intro: {Journalist Name} <> {Your Name}

```
Hey {Journalist Name},

Just read your article on {Topic}! AMAZING stuff, had to intro you to my 
friend {Your Name} (CC'd) who {brief description of what you did}...

{Link to your content}

Take it from here!
{Alias Name}
```

**Rules for alias emails:**
- Make typos on purpose (looks authentic, creates follow-up opportunities)
- Keep it 1-5 lines max
- Use female names (tested higher open rates)
- Track link clicks with hidden Bitly (paste full URL but hyperlink to Bitly)
- Sometimes pretend you've met before ("We met at {generic local venue} a few weeks back")

### The Follow-Up Chain

1. **Day 0:** Alias sends intro email (CC'ing you)
2. **Day 1-2:** If opened but no reply → you reply: "Thanks {Alias}! Moving you to BCC. Hey {Journalist}, would love to talk. Would Wednesday work?"
3. **Day 3-4:** If still no reply → Alias follows up: "Hey {Journalist}, did you get a chance to connect with {Your Name}?"
4. **Fallback subject line:** "RE: Still want to talk? Or no?" — creates social pressure because not replying now equals saying "no"

## Journalist Hunting & Database Building

### Finding Journalists Who Will Care

1. Google your topic
2. Click "News" tab
3. Filter by "Past 24 hours"
4. Every journalist listed just wrote about your topic — they're warm

### Building Your Database

Create a spreadsheet with columns:
- Journalist First Name
- Publication
- Topic they wrote about
- Email address

### Finding Email Addresses
1. **Twitter bios** — most journalists list their email
2. **LinkedIn** — check contact info, or Ctrl+F for "@"
3. **LinkedIn contact export** — add journalists as connections, download contacts CSV → get personal emails
4. **Email pattern guessing** — find one employee's email format (firstname.lastname@company.com), apply to all
5. **Hunter.io** — Chrome extension that scans page source for email addresses and shows common patterns

### Mass Outreach via Mail Merge
- Use YAMM (Yet Another Mail Merge) on Google Sheets
- Send thousands of personalized emails in minutes
- Auto-populate fields: first name, publication, recent article topic
- Example: 2,000+ personalized emails to entertainment journalists in 2 minutes for #KardBlock

## Email Tracking & Manipulation

### Streak (Gmail Extension)
- Track when emails are read, what device, how many times, what location
- Gauge interest: read 8 times but no reply = interested but hesitant
- Identify timezone issues: journalist reads at midnight their time → re-send at their morning

### The "Cosmic Intervention" Technique
- Set Streak to notify you the INSTANT someone reads your email
- Reply within 60 seconds
- Recipient is amazed at the "coincidence" — feels like real-time connection
- People feel more obligated to reply to real-time interactions
- Hat: `[GREY]` — manipulative but effective

## Viral Seeding Channels

### Forums
- Google "{your topic} forums" → join the top 25
- Post with alias accounts (never self-promote)
- Reddit is critical — journalists at BuzzFeed, Elite Daily, Daily Dot monitor it 24/7
- Post in multiple relevant subreddits/categories

### Blogs & Facebook Pages
- Target content distribution pages (not brand pages)
- Message student-run pages, niche community pages
- Use open-ended questions in posts to drive engagement
- Leave argumentative comments from secondary accounts to boost algorithm visibility

### Facebook Groups
- Database relevant groups
- Join with alias accounts
- Post content with engagement-driving questions
- Comment from real/secondary accounts to start dialogue
- More engagement = more algorithmic favor

## Trading Up the Chain

Small coverage → medium coverage → major coverage.

1. Get small blogs to write about you first
2. Larger blogs monitor smaller ones → they'll discover you
3. If they don't discover you organically → email them the small blog articles as "tips"
4. **Nuclear option:** If no small blog will write about you first, write about yourself with an alias account on Medium/Hacker Noon, then send THAT article to journalists as the seed

## Viral Nets — Capturing the Burst

Going viral without capture systems is wasted energy. Before going viral, set up:

1. **Email capture** — landing page with email opt-in (the Puppify model: 3,000 emails for $5)
2. **Facebook Pixel** — installed on all landing pages for retargeting and lookalike audiences
3. **Google Analytics** — track everything about your viral visitors
4. **Social follows** — push viral traffic to follow your social accounts
5. **Waiting lists** — if product isn't ready, capture intent for later

## When NOT to Go Viral

Going viral doesn't make sense if:
- **Geographically constrained** — a local garage can't serve global traffic
- **Can't scale** — if you max out at 60% capacity, a traffic spike wastes
- **Product isn't ready** — dating app with no users = people download, see empty app, delete, tell friends it sucks
- **Goal is long-term brand equity** — viral is a spike, not sustained growth
- **Goal is product quality** — viral attention forces you into support mode, not development mode

Going viral DOES make sense if:
- Goal is brand awareness, lead generation, or rapid user acquisition
- Service is digital and globally deliverable
- You can scale to meet demand
- You have viral nets in place to capture the burst
- You want to impress investors with numbers (careful: spike then decline)

## The Power Dynamic Flip

**Advanced technique from real-world application:**

Instead of "we want something FROM you" (sales pitch), flip to "we're doing something FOR you" (protective service).

Example: Self-storage brokerage ad targeting facility owners sick of broker calls.
- Instead of: "Let us sell your property" (what every broker says)
- Flipped to: "Other agents might already be listing your property for sale. Apply and our AI platform will check."
- Owners filled in details out of FEAR (trigger #5) someone was selling their property without permission
- Call: "Good news, your property isn't listed. Were you thinking about selling?"
- Door opened naturally. Trust already established.

**Principle:** Frame your lead generation as a service to the prospect. Make them feel like they're getting value, not being sold to. The information you collect "for them" is the lead data you need.
